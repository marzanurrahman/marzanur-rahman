The Problem

Running Ads Is Easy. Building a Repeatable Acquisition Process Is Harder.

Campaign performance often becomes difficult to understand when strategy, creative decisions and measurement are treated as separate activities.

01

Unclear Campaign Direction

Campaigns launch without a clearly defined acquisition objective or testing priority.

02

Creative Fatigue

The same messages and creatives continue running after performance begins to decline.

03

Weak Measurement

Leads are generated, but campaign data is disconnected from actual customer outcomes and the wider acquisition process.

04

Reactive Optimisation

Decisions are made from short-term performance changes instead of structured analysis and continuous learning.

The Approach

How I Approach Meta Marketing

The work starts by understanding the business and acquisition process before campaign decisions are made.

01

Business & Offer Review

Understand the business, offer, customer and current acquisition process before defining the advertising direction.

02

Campaign Strategy

Define campaign objectives, audience direction, budget structure and testing priorities around the business goal.

03

Creative Direction

Develop messaging angles, creative concepts and testing variations around customer problems, intent and campaign objectives.

04

Campaign Execution

Build and launch campaigns with a clear structure for delivery, testing and measurement.

05

Performance & Iteration

Review campaign and creative data, identify performance changes and introduce new tests or adjustments when needed.

Creative Strategy

Creative Is Part of the Campaign Strategy.

Creative decisions should not be separated from campaign performance. I review previous campaign and creative data to understand what has been working, where performance may be declining and what new messaging or creative direction should be tested next.
